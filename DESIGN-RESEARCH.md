# Design research: OutsourcedPhilippines.com

## Search intent and guardrails

The working query was `Philippines outsourcing company Filipino staffing`. The homepage is for companies considering Filipino talent sourced only from the Philippines. It is an independent lead-generation guide, so the copy must not imply that the site directly employs staff. Public pricing, borrowed assets, invented reviews, and unsupported performance claims are out.

Research was completed on July 21, 2026. Each competitor below was opened and visually inspected in a desktop browser. Notes describe what appeared in the inspected capture; blank or faint areas may have been lazy-loading or animation artifacts.

## Five direct competitors

### 1. Cloudstaff

URL: https://www.cloudstaff.com/

Screenshot: `/home/hermes/.hermes/cache/screenshots/browser_screenshot_66494ed6e6b044418ca277db22a29378.png`

Cloudstaff uses a pale blue-to-purple hero with a cutout staff portrait, floating technology symbols, heavy geometric type, and several proof layers near the top. The role taxonomy is easy to scan, and the repeated employee portraits make a large company feel more human. Its strongest lesson is the immediate pairing of a person with operational proof. The page is very long, the early newsletter competes with the sales action, and CTA wording changes often. A large cookie panel covered part of the hero in the inspected capture.

### 2. Outsourced.ph

URL: https://outsourced.ph/

Screenshot: `/home/hermes/.hermes/cache/screenshots/browser_screenshot_fb05afba4e41407caf57ddb08b7e6bd1.png`

Outsourced.ph has a confident navy, cyan, and magenta system. Its hero combines a short staffing proposition with a friendly line illustration. Benefit tiles, a four-step process, service cards, media mentions, workplace photography, and a strong contact section cover the buyer journey. The color hierarchy is clear, but the page repeats rounded cards and uses several labels for the main sales action. The cookie notice obscured much of the first screen during inspection.

### 3. Booth & Partners

URL: https://boothandpartners.com/

Screenshot: `/home/hermes/.hermes/cache/screenshots/browser_screenshot_0ceacbe9995443fdbaba9351cf68c2b3.png`

Booth was the strongest visual direction. Its deep forest hero, pale lilac action, candid team photo, oversized type, hand-drawn line motif, and floating metric cards feel closer to a premium advisory firm than a staffing directory. Large-radius cards and warm off-white surfaces continue that tone. The page also explains process and governance rather than relying on one trust strip. Booth serves multiple regions; that is a factual competitor difference, not a model for this site. OutsourcedPhilippines.com remains Philippines-only. Several lower-page counters and resource areas appeared unloaded in the full-page capture.

### 4. Remote Staff

URL: https://www.remotestaff.com.au/

Screenshot: `/home/hermes/.hermes/cache/screenshots/browser_screenshot_65de58e7cea34732abee13b3be2c7ab5.png`

Remote Staff pairs dark forest green and electric lime with editorial serif headings. Its most useful module is the operational card set for onboarding, scoping, replacement, and performance review. The savings calculator is distinctive, though this redesign will not use public pricing or savings claims. Much of the inspected page was faint or blank, which looked like a loading or animation state rather than a safe pattern to copy.

### 5. Clark Staff

URL: https://clarkstaff.com/

Screenshot: `/home/hermes/.hermes/cache/screenshots/browser_screenshot_f907aded94194cb3bdc31e289629049a.png`

Clark Staff uses a direct blue hero, a huge white headline, burgundy actions, numerical proof cards, service explanations, and long testimonial blocks. The category is obvious within seconds, and the repeated discovery-call action is consistent. The visual system gets repetitive over the long page, while several large blank areas appeared to be missing or unloaded media. Its broad talent language also does not fit this site's Philippines-only rule.

## Chosen direction

Booth & Partners won for its premium people-first composition, forest-and-lilac palette, expressive headline scale, candid workplace photography, and process depth. The useful design logic is:

- treat the hero like an editorial cover rather than a generic card grid;
- place a real working scene beside the proposition;
- use one clear action throughout;
- show operating documents and decision boundaries as proof;
- let cards vary in size and purpose instead of repeating one component.

## Original implementation

The built concept is the **Philippines role room**. It does not copy Booth's wording, logo, photographs, layout, or metrics. The homepage uses an original folded role-brief logo, a locally stored Unsplash workplace image, a dark role-room hero, a lavender role slip, coral accents, a PH-only seal, and ruled planning modules. The work desk, access-boundary card, starting lanes, process ledger, and handoff notes are specific to planning Filipino roles.

The hero image shows colleagues discussing work around monitors in an open office. It does not establish anyone's nationality, so the alt text stays factual. Nearby copy states that the staffing model sources Filipino talent only from the Philippines.

The exact handoff marker is `data-design="philippines-role-room-2026-07"`. The exact H1 is `Build a Filipino team around the work you already know.`

## Content and originality notes

All visitor-facing copy was rewritten in a plain, conversational voice. The page avoids generic claims about transformation, global talent, public prices, savings, rankings, ratings, testimonials, and client logos. Trust comes from process detail: a working brief, an approval line, access rules, a named reviewer, and a realistic first lane. The CTA is `Request a role brief` from header to closing section. The independent routing disclosure sits near the hero action and on the form.

Competitor screenshots and assets are research evidence only. None are included in the repository or public site.
